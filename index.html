Project Objectives & Scope

The Problem

In Malaysia, the Ministry of Health is actively battling rising obesity rates. While many Malaysians are taking up running, they face "Gadget Fatigue"‚Äîthe inconvenience of carrying smartwatches, phones, and trackers that run out of battery or are uncomfortable[cite: 19, 44].

Our Solution: Smart Shoes

We propose a wearable technology that integrates fitness tracking and safety features directly into footwear. This reduces the risk of injury and eliminates the need for multiple devices[cite: 28, 48].

"To design an innovative wearable technology that integrates multiple fitness tracking and safety features into a single product." [cite: 51]

Methodology & Best Work Samples

We utilized critical thinking tools such as Empathy Mapping, Fishbone Diagrams, and the 5W1H approach to deeply understand our users[cite: 52].

Artifact 1: Empathy Map Findings

We analyzed user personas to find hidden pain points:

  • Eden Shah (User): Finds daily charging "troublesome" and worries about back pain from poor posture[cite: 144, 161].
  • Suriani Salmi (User): Wants shoes that are anti-odour and auto-sizing[cite: 125].
  • Khairul Anwar (User): Often forgets to bring his smartwatch, leading to unrecorded runs[cite: 129].
Artifact 2: Problem Statement

Core Issue: Inconvenience & Safety

Existing devices cause discomfort and dependency on battery life. Users forget to charge gadgets, resulting in incomplete data[cite: 46].

Innovation Strategy

By moving the tech *into* the shoe, we create a "Hands-free" gadget that is more accurate for step counting and posture monitoring[cite: 104].

Artifact 3: Design Features

Based on our QADIM creative thinking session, the final product features:

  • Safety: Real-time posture alerts to prevent injury[cite: 160].
  • Convenience: 5-7 Day battery life target[cite: 180].
  • Versatility: Suitable for different road surfaces and sports[cite: 77].
